Overview

Why this strategy matters now

Why now

Keele University is entering a period in which clarity of mission, disciplined delivery and a strong sense of institutional identity matter more than ever. The strategy provides a shared framework for making decisions across education, research, partnership, investment and culture.

Who it is for

It is intended to be readable by different audiences. Staff need a line of sight from university-wide ambition to local planning. Students and applicants need to understand what kind of institution Keele is becoming. Partners, communities and alumni need to see how the university will create value with and for them over time.

How it works

The strategy therefore combines a clear narrative with practical intent. It identifies what the university will prioritise, what foundations must be strengthened, and how progress will be tracked so the document does not sit apart from the daily life of the institution.

Vision and purpose

Opportunity, knowledge and public value

Opportunity and purpose

Our university exists to create opportunity through education, generate knowledge with purpose and contribute to the prosperity and wellbeing of the communities we serve. This strategy sets out how we will strengthen that purpose over the next decade and make it more visible in the choices we take.

Values and direction

We will be values-led, evidence-informed and partnership-minded. We will act with confidence in our strengths while remaining open to change, collaboration and challenge. The intention is not to do more of everything, but to do the most important things with greater coherence and impact.

Keele identity

Keele’s identity has always been shaped by breadth, interdisciplinarity, campus community and a strong relationship to place. The next phase of development builds on those characteristics while adapting to a world in which higher education is expected to be more agile, more accountable and more explicit about the difference it makes.

Context

Responding to a more demanding environment

Sector pressures

Higher education is facing a period of profound transition. Student expectations continue to evolve. Financial pressures are increasing across the sector. Digital change is affecting how people learn, teach, research and work. Employers and communities are asking universities to demonstrate more clearly how knowledge is translated into opportunity and impact.

Strategic choice

In this context, institutional success depends on a sharper understanding of where we can make the greatest contribution. Strategy should not become a catalogue of aspirations. It should be a disciplined response to context, grounded in evidence about strengths, capabilities, risks and emerging opportunities.

Keele response

Keele’s response is to focus deliberately on educational distinctiveness, purposeful research, partnership with place, and the enabling conditions that support long-term resilience. The strategy also recognises that a clearer institutional story improves not only reputation but decision making: it helps the university choose where to invest, where to collaborate and what to stop doing.

Education priority

Education that opens futures

Student experience

Keele will create an educational experience that is intellectually challenging, inclusive by design and connected to the needs of a changing world. Students will benefit from strong disciplinary foundations, richer interdisciplinary opportunities and clearer pathways into employment, further study and lifelong learning.

What we will invest in

This requires continued investment in curriculum renewal, teaching quality and student experience. It also requires a more coherent end-to-end student journey, from recruitment and transition through to progression, belonging, graduation and alumni connection. The strategy therefore links educational ambition with support, digital capability and clearer communication about what makes a Keele education distinctive.

What success looks like

Over time, success in this area will be visible through stronger student outcomes, more flexible routes into and through study, and an educational offer that feels relevant to the complexity of contemporary work and citizenship.

Research priority

Research and innovation with reach

Reach and relevance

Research at Keele will be recognised not only for quality but also for reach. The university will back areas of established and emerging strength, encourage collaboration across boundaries and build clearer pathways from discovery to application, influence and public benefit.

Capability and concentration

A successful research strategy needs both concentration and connectivity. It must concentrate resources where talent and institutional advantage are strongest, while also connecting people, infrastructure and partnerships in ways that make ambitious work possible. This includes stronger support for talent, funding development, interdisciplinary collaboration and external engagement.

Routes to impact

The intended outcome is a research environment in which ideas travel further: into policy, industry, civic life, culture and professional practice. That means valuing translation and engagement alongside traditional measures of excellence.

Partnership priority

Partnership, place and public value

Relationships that matter

Keele’s ambitions will be realised through relationships. The strategy commits the university to deeper collaboration with employers, schools, health systems, public services, cultural organisations, alumni and community partners so that teaching, research and engagement create broader public value.

Partnership as core work

This is not an additional strand of activity sitting apart from the university’s core work. Partnership is how the institution becomes more effective in education and research. It shapes the opportunities students encounter, the routes through which research has impact, and the way the university contributes to local and regional prosperity.

Anchor institution role

The strategy therefore positions Keele as an anchor institution that is outward-facing, collaborative and accountable to place while remaining connected to national and international networks.

Delivery

Delivery, accountability and renewal

Governance and measures

Strategy matters only when it shapes priorities, choices and behaviours. Keele will support delivery through transparent governance, a manageable set of institutional measures, regular reporting and a clearer line of sight between institutional goals and local planning.

Capacity for delivery

Delivery also requires capacity: leadership, culture, digital and data capability, financial discipline and the willingness to review progress honestly. The university will therefore treat implementation as an active process of learning and adjustment rather than a one-off launch moment.

Review and renewal

Over the lifetime of the strategy, progress will be revisited regularly so priorities can be refreshed in response to changing circumstances. That adaptive approach is essential if the strategy is to remain a living framework for institutional direction rather than a static statement of intent.
